Garden design

Andrew Judge designed the Indigenous Garden in the shape of a semicircle around a spiral. The spiral represents the basis of all life and it brings and creates energy in the garden.

Action

Plants native to North America

In this activity, you will design an Indigenous Garden using plants that are native to North America.

Test Your Skills

Design Your Garden

Choose 5 different types of plants that are native to North America. Plant 10 seeds of the same plant in each of the 5 rows around the spiral in the garden. You can draw your seeds in the garden using the template provided or use different materials provided by your teacher. You will then count how many seeds you planted and show how you counted them.

Complete the Indigenous Garden Template in your notebook or using the following fillable and printable document.

Press the “Printable” button to access the template. Label your garden’s plants.

Skip counting activity

  • Can you grow a plant from the local area at home?
  • What will it need to survive?
  • Can you design an Indigenous Garden with 75 seeds?
  • How many rows would you use?

Create a necklace using 50 beads with different colours or textures, 50 pieces of cereal or 50 pieces of popcorn. Group beads of the same type together (for example, beads of the same texture, size, shape or colour) into groups of 1, 2, 5 or 10. Continue the skip counting pattern until you reach 50 beads in total.

How did you count the beads? Represent your skip counting pattern using drawings or make circles with bingo dabbers, markers or crayons.
